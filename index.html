How to read these results

Xpeak is the concentration giving maximum ternary complex formation — this is the headline ranking number. It is computed numerically from the fitted curve.

EC50 (apparent) and IC50 (apparent) are empirical fit parameters describing where the curve rises and where it falls. They are not binding constants (Kd) — they should not be reported as such.

Width (nM) is the linear distance between the two inflection points (IC50 − EC50). Width (fold) is the same gap as a fold ratio (IC50 / EC50) — scale-invariant and easier to compare across compounds with very different potencies.

Hill1, Hill2 are slope parameters for the rising and falling sides of the bell. R² is goodness-of-fit on the supplied data points — note that with few data points and 6 free parameters, R² can sit very close to 1 even when the parameters are uncertain. The real diagnostics are the standard errors and the At bound column.

Rows highlighted in pink indicate likely-bad fits: failed convergence, low R², or one or more parameters pinned at a bound (e.g. Hill = 4 means the optimizer wanted a steeper slope than the model permits).

The model fitted is a product of two sigmoids (ascending × hook) with IC50 > EC50 enforced — fitted independently per sample with no shared parameters. The optimizer runs from 10 different starting points and keeps the best fit, so noisy data is much less likely to cause a stuck or unstable result.

Replicates: if multiple columns share the same sample name (e.g. columns B, C, D all named "Compound A"), they are treated as replicates of the same sample and fit together as a single curve. This is much more stable than fitting each replicate separately. The "N pts" column shows how many individual data points each fit used.

Per-sample fits

Model: bell-shaped dose–response — product of two Hill sigmoids (one ascending for ternary complex formation, one descending for the high-concentration hook). Each sample below is fit independently with this equation.
Y(X) = Bottom + (Top − Bottom) · [ (X / EC50)Hill1 / (1 + (X / EC50)Hill1) ] · [ 1 / (1 + (X / IC50)Hill2) ]
Six free parameters per curve: Bottom, Top, EC50, IC50, Hill1, Hill2. Fitted by Levenberg–Marquardt nonlinear least squares with the constraint IC50 > EC50 enforced.
